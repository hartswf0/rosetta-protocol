A Stuart Hall-Inspired Analysis of the Jimmy Kimmel Affair

About this Analysis

This document explores the September 2025 Jimmy Kimmel suspension and return through the theoretical lens of Stuart Hall. It treats the event not as a simple news story, but as a "conjuncture"—a moment where complex social forces collide. The analysis models the media coverage as a "dialectic of cultural struggle," identifying moves of **containment** by the power-bloc and acts of **resistance** by popular forces. A Stuart Hall-Inspired Analysis of the Jimmy Kimmel Affair

The Conjuncture

ABC’s suspension and reinstatement of Kimmel amid pressure from Trump-aligned actors triggered a cross-platform fight over free-speech norms and institutional authority.

1. Periodisation

“Difficult problems are posed here by périodisation... Are the major breaks largely descriptive? Do they arise... from factors which are outside of but impinge on it?”

Hall demands we identify the key historical "breaks" where the rules of the struggle change, driven by external pressures.

The Breaks in Play

Breakpoint 1: Containment

(Sep 15–17) External political pressure leads to a corporate disciplinary action (the suspension). The power-bloc actively pushes Kimmel's expression to the margins.

Breakpoint 2: Resistance

(Sep 23–24) Kimmel’s return, amplified by a massive digital audience bypassing TV blackouts, constitutes a qualitative change in the "apparatus" of distribution.

2. The 'Popular'

“What is essential... is the relations which define ‘popular culture’ in a continuing tension (relationship, influence and antagonism) to the dominant culture.”

For Hall, "popular culture" is not a set of things, but a battlefield defined by the struggle between the power-bloc and "the people."

A Struggle Over Definition

Power-Bloc Definition

Kimmel's speech is framed as dangerous, illegitimate, and outside the bounds of acceptable broadcast content. The goal is to **contain** it.

Popular Forces' Definition

Kimmel's speech is framed as necessary satire, a core part of a democratic tradition. The goal is to **resist** containment.

3. The Dialectic of Struggle

“We should always start here: with the double-stake in popular culture, the double movement of containment and resistance, which is always inevitably inside it.”

Culture is a constant push-and-pull. The powerful try to contain and manage culture, while popular forces resist and push back.

Mechanisms of the Struggle

Mechanisms of Containment

  • Ban/Proscription: Affiliate blackouts.
  • Policing: FCC threats.
  • Incorporation: Framing Kimmel as an out-of-touch elite.

Mechanisms of Resistance

  • Viral Bypass: Using YouTube to circumvent the ban.
  • Appropriation: Reframing the debate around the First Amendment.
  • Coalition Building: Uniting entertainers and free-speech advocates.
